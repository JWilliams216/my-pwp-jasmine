Children

Spending quality time with children can be challenging, especially with busy schedules and the demands of daily life. However, with some planning and creativity, it is possible to make the most of the time you have with your children.

Bedtime Routine
  • Set a consistent bedtime: regulate their inner clock
  • Wind down: 15-30 minutes prior
  • Avoid stimulating activities: No roughhousing or screen time
  • Dim lights: creating a calm and restful atmosphere
  • Establish a sleep-friendly environment: dark, quiet, white noise
  • Limit food and drink: especially sugar and large meals
  • Bath or shower: calming their body
  • Read and cuddle
Importance of Reading Together
  • Language development: Reading aloud to your child helps develop their language skills by exposing them to new words, sentence structures, and ideas. This helps them develop a larger vocabulary and improve their communication skills.
  • Cognitive development: Reading helps develop a child's cognitive abilities by improving their concentration, memory, and critical thinking skills. Children learn to recognize patterns, understand cause-and-effect relationships, and make connections between different ideas.
  • Emotional development: Reading can help children understand and express their emotions by providing them with characters and stories that they can relate to. This can help children develop empathy and social skills.
  • Academic success: Studies have shown that children who are read to regularly perform better academically. Reading helps develop literacy skills such as phonemic awareness, letter recognition, and comprehension, which are essential for academic success.
  • Bonding: Reading aloud to your child can be a special time for bonding and creating positive memories. This can help strengthen the parent-child relationship and create a love of reading that can last a lifetime.

Spending time outdoors can help children develop an appreciation for nature, and provide opportunities for physical activity and exploration.

Nature scavenger hunt: Create a list of items to search for such as leaves, rocks, flowers, or animal tracks. Children can explore their surroundings and check off each item they find.

Picnic in the park: Pack a lunch or snacks and enjoy a picnic in a local park or nature preserve. Children can play on the playground, go for a walk, or simply enjoy the outdoors.

Gardening: Start a small garden or plant some flowers with children. This activity provides an opportunity to learn about plant growth and responsibility for taking care of plants.

Outdoor games: Play classic outdoor games like hide-and-seek, tag, or kickball. These games promote physical activity and are a fun way to spend time outdoors.

Bird watching: Bring a pair of binoculars and a bird identification book and observe the different types of birds in the area. Children can learn about bird behavior, migration, and habitat.

Camping: Plan a camping trip or backyard camping experience. Children can help set up a tent, cook food over a campfire or grill, and learn about outdoor safety.

Household

Organizing

  • Break into small manageable steps.
  • De-clutter: Go through each room removing items you no longer need or belong in other rooms.
  • Return items to designated place.
  • Use labeled storage solutions: Shelves, baskets, drawers.
  • Keep surfaces clear: Tabletops, counters, and tops of dressers.

Commitments

  • Make a list of all your commitments, no matter how small: Work, personal, and family obligations.
  • Decided which commitments you can eliminate: It's okay to say no and not be able to fulfill all commitments that come across your plate.
  • Prioritize with numbers: Which need to happen that day and which can be done within the week.
  • Break up larger commitments into smaller more manageable tasks.
  • Delegate when possible.

Chores

  • Make a list of all chores.
  • Break larger chores into smaller tasks if needed.
  • Determine how often each chore needs to be done: Daily, weekly, monthly, or a couple of times a year.
  • Figure out how long it takes to accomplish each chore: Time yourself doing each chore and keep a record.
  • Assign each chore to a specific day or time of week.
  • Use a chore chart or add to your calendar in different color to keep yourself on track throughout the week.

Schedule

  • Box Method: It's flexible and can be adjusted to fit each day.
  • Use Google Keep or time blocking apps.
  • Box off when you wake up and go to sleep.
  • Box fixed commitments: When you go to work, kids go to school or daycare, and appointments.
  • Box high-priority tasks: Things that need to be done that day that are most important to your goals and responsibilities.
  • Box high-priority tasks: Tasks or activities that you enjoy, such as reading, exercise, time with friends.

Mental Health & Self Care

Support & Community

Your support network is made up of individuals who offer emotional, practical, or professional support, such as family, friends, coworkers, mental health professionals, and support groups. These individuals provide a listening ear, advice, and assistance when needed, which can help you manage stress and improve your well-being.

Building and maintaining a support network involves reaching out to existing relationships, seeking new connections, and actively engaging with your network to develop strong and meaningful relationships that meet your needs.

Peer-led Support Groups

Peer-led support groups are run by individuals who have experienced mental health issues themselves. These groups provide a supportive environment where members can share their experiences, provide emotional support, and learn from each other.

Pediatric Nutritionist

Pediatric Nutritionists specialize in optimizing nutrition for children and addressing their unique needs and challenges. They provide guidance on optimal nutrition for supporting healthy growth and development.

  • Assessing a child's growth patterns and ensure they are meeting appropriate milestones.
  • Creating balanced meal plans tailored to children's needs and preferences. They consider factors such as age, gender, activity level, and specific dietary requirements or restrictions.
  • Addressing picky eating behaviors and feeding challenges, providing strategies to introduce new foods, improve variety, and ensure adequate nutrient intake.
  • Providing guidance on weight management including appropriate portion sizes, balanced meals, and fostering a positive relationship with food.

Holistic Nutritionist

A Holistic Nutritionist takes a comprehensive approach to nutrition, considering the interconnectedness of the body, mind, and spirit.

  • Personalized nutrition assessments and meal planning that support your body's natural healing processes and promote overall wellness.
  • Supporting stress management and emotional well-being such as incorporating relaxation techniques, mindfulness practices, and mood-balancing foods.
  • Recommending functional foods and supplements to address specific health concerns or optimize nutritional status.
  • Providing education and support to empower you to make informed choices for holistic health goals.

They focus on promoting overall well-being and balance that consider your unique requirements and preferences.

General Practitioner

  • Are there lifestyle changes or self-care strategies that can help improve my mental health?
  • What steps can I take to manage my mental health and prevent future mental health issues?
  • What can I expect during a mental health evaluation or treatment session?
  • Can you provide me with information about mental health resources and support groups in my area?
  • Are there any potential side effects or risks associated with mental health treatment?

Accident & Emergency Medicine

  • What can I expect during my stay in the A&E department for mental health treatment?
  • Can you provide me with a mental health assessment or evaluation?
  • Can you refer me to a mental health specialist, such as a psychiatrist or therapist, for ongoing treatment and support?
  • Are there any immediate treatment options available for mental health conditions, such as medication or crisis counseling?
  • What is the aftercare plan for mental health treatment, and what follow-up care should I expect?

Pediatrician

  • Are there any lifestyle changes or self-care strategies that can improve my child's mental health and reduce stress?
  • How can I support my child's emotional well-being and foster a positive mental health environment at home?
  • When should I seek professional help for my child's mental health, and what treatment options are available?
  • Can you refer me to a mental health specialist, such as a child psychiatrist or therapist, if my child needs additional support?
  • Are there any resources or support groups for parents of children with mental health issues or stress?

In-person therapy.

This is the traditional way of therapy where you meet with a therapist in their office or a clinical setting. In-person therapy allows for face-to-face communication, which can help build rapport and trust between the therapist and client

Group therapy

Group therapy involves meeting with a therapist and a group of individuals who are experiencing similar issues. Group therapy can be a good option for those who benefit from peer support and can also be more affordable than individual therapy.

Text-based Therapy

Some therapists offer therapy sessions through text-based communication, such as messaging or email. This can be a good option for those who prefer to communicate in writing or who have difficulty expressing themselves verbally.

Teletherapy

Teletherapy is remote therapy conducted through video chat or phone calls, allowing people to attend sessions from anywhere with an internet connection. This makes it a convenient option for those with mobility issues, living in remote areas, or struggling to leave their homes.
